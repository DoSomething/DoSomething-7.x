What is Grandparents Gone Wired?

Grandparents Gone Wired urges young people to share their tech knowledge with their grandparents or seniors in their community to help the senior learn to use new forms of technology.

How does this work?

It’s simple! Pick an area (or 3) of expertise, and share your knowledge with grandparents, elderly family members/friends or seniors living in your local senior home who might like to learn how to use technology to make their lives easier. When you are done teaching, tell us all about it to be entered to win a $5000 scholarship. You can download this Guide for some helpful tips for teaching.

What is Intel?

Intel is an American technology company that is best known for creating microprocessors for computers.

Why is Intel involved with Grandparents Gone Wired?

Intel’s mission is to create and extend computing technology to connect and enrich the lives of every person on earth. As a result, they partnered with us to help connect seniors and teens across the country.

What do we get for doing this?

Besides the feeling that you have helped make someone else’s life better, we’ll be giving away a $5000 scholarship.

Do I have to teach my senior how to use Facebook, Skype, and the general computer skills to participate?

Nope! You can teach them one or all three—it’s up to you.

How can I win the scholarship?

There’s two ways to qualify 1) share a stat about seniors and technology to 5 friends 2) teach a senior how to use Facebook, Skype, and their computer and tell us about it. If you do both actions you’ll DOUBLE your chances of winning the $5,000 scholarship.Simply answer the questions on the quick and easy Tell Us About It form. Recipients will be determined by DoSomething.org’ Scholarships Team. Please visit the Scholarships Rules for more info.
