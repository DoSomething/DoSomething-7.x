Frequently Asked Questions  

What is Grandparents Gone Wired?

Grandparents Gone Wired encourages young people to share their tech knowledge with their grandparents or older adults in their community to help the older adult learn to use new forms of technology and stay connected with friends and family.

How does this work?

First, pick a type of technology (link to tech section) you’d like to teach to an older adult. Get together with that person, and follow the teaching tips for that particular technology. Report back with a photo of you and the older adult using the technology and you’ll be entered to win the $4,000 scholarship.

You can also enter to win other sweet prizes. Check out the challenges above.

Do I have to teach every type of technology to an older adult?

Absolutely not! It’s important not only to make sure you are comfortable teaching the technology, but that the older adult wants to learn it as well. Make sure you take time to ask them what they’d most like to do (check email, talk to people over video chat, send text messages, etc.). Then you can check out the specific teaching tips for each section.

If you’d like to teach a form of technology that’s not on our website, go for it! Email Sarah at grandparents@dosomething.org if you have any questions.

And remember, to enter to win the $4,000, report back with a photo of you and the older adult learning the technology.

What if I don’t know any older adults?

No problem! Older adults at senior centers are always looking for ways to learn new technology.

The easiest way to find a local senior center is to search online for your town + “senior center” or “nursing home.” Usually a phone number/address will pop up.

If you don’t get any results that way, try contacting your town officials or places of worship (like churches or synagogues) to get info. You can also try to find your local senior center through your local Department of Aging.

Who is Mentor Up?

Mentor Up is a new way to make a difference from AARP Foundation that brings together the younger generation's energy and talents to pursue reverse mentoring and community service opportunities that help people 50 and older.

In addition, Mentor Up is also working with organizations rooted in service and driven by social missions, including the 4-H Council, DoSomething.org, Generations United, The Intergenerational Center at Temple University, Magic Johnson Foundation and Facebook. For more information about Mentor Up, visit www.mentorup.org.

How do I win the $4,000 scholarship?

To enter to win the scholarship, simply report back by submitting a photo of you and your grandparent (or the older adult) you helped connect to technology while you are teaching (hint -- you and grandpa in front of a computer, you and a woman at a senior center learning to send a text message). This will give you one entry into the scholarship.

Every extra person you help you will be entered to win the scholarship again. For example, if you help 10 people you will be entered to win the scholarship 10 times! You can do this by submitting 1 photo that proves you helped 10 people at once, or by reporting back 10 times with 10 different people in the photo.
