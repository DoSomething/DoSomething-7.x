What’s this game about?

Great leaders, like Martin Luther King Jr., fought for equal rights, it took many individuals risking their lives to change legal discrimination in this country. Students led many of the protests and direct action that made immediate changes to discriminatory laws. This game puts you in the shoes of these unknown individuals who risked their lives to change their communities or the better. Challenge your friends and you’ll also qualify for a scholarship.

Why should I participate?

You’ll learn the many ways activism changed our country, and how those same tactics can be used now. Challenge your friends and you’ll also qualify for a scholarship.

What is segregation?

To get technical it’s the “separation of human beings into racial groups in daily life” To put it simply, laws were used to keep groups separate and to oppress minorities. These laws are no longer in place, but discrimination still exists.

When did legal segregation end?

The Civil Rights Act of 1965 banned legal segregation, but it took many years for segregation.
