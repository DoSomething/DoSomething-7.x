Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I do this?

Senior Citizens frequently fight depression due to isolation. After the holidays and families return home, this isolation is maximized. Help brighten a senior in your communities day by sending them a Valentines Day card.

I’m having trouble coming up with an idea for my card. Help!

For some creative card ideas, check out our action guides here.

Why am I sending my card to Meals on Wheels?

Meals on Wheels provides over one million meals to senior citizens each day. Their goals are to help seniors maintain independence as they age and enable them to live in their own homes. Meals on Wheels programs across the country are able to hand deliver your cards to senior citizens nationwide. Find a program to send your card to here.

What is Mentor Up?

Mentor Up is a new way to make a difference from AARP Foundation that brings together the younger generation's energy and talents to pursue reverse mentoring and community service opportunities that help people 50 and older.

In addition, Mentor Up is also working with organizations rooted in service and driven by social missions, including the 4-H Council, DoSomething.org, Generations United, The Intergenerational Center at Temple University, Magic Johnson Foundation and Facebook. For more information about Mentor Up, visit www.mentorup.org.

What happens after I send my card?

Once you mail your card to a Meals on Wheels program, they will be received by a staff member and distributed to an older adult in their region.

There is an older adult in my town I want to give it to. Can I send it there?

Yes, just be sure to keep your personal information anonymous. Our guide here can show you how.

How do I win the $4,000 scholarship?

The scholarship winner will be picked randomly. To enter the lottery, make and send a card. To be entered again, make three more cards. There is no limit on the number of times you can enter. Don’t forget to tell us about the cards you make -- that’s the only way we will know how many times to enter you into the lottery. Plus, when you report back your photos will appear in the gallery!

I don’t live in the U.S., can I still participate?

Yea! To do so, find an organization in your area that works with older adults and then ask if they would like to receive cards from you. If there isn’t an organization in your area or country, feel free to choose one on our list and mail your card(s) to the U.S.
